# Al Haras Company Founding Brief

## BLUF

Al Haras is HYBA's sovereign-grade security company. It is distinct from the internal AL HARAS runtime module, but it is built on the same HYBA mathematical substrate.

The company thesis is structural: HYBA's substrate-agnostic, mathematically sovereign, hardware-independent execution layer is compelling not only for finance and healthcare, but also for national security, critical infrastructure, and sovereign AI protection.

Al Haras should be positioned as a defensive, high-assurance security company for HYBA, sovereign clients, and regulated national-security-adjacent infrastructure.

## Leadership note

Al Haras is expected to be led by David Medalia.

## Company boundary

Al Haras the company owns sovereign-grade security products, security assurance, protected execution, critical-infrastructure protection, classified-environment readiness, and high-assurance defensive deployment pathways.

Al Haras the company does not own HYBA-wide governance and compliance. That belongs to Themis.

Themis remains the governance and compliance layer for jurisdiction mapping, regulatory obligations, risk classification, explainability, documentation, human oversight, regulator-facing evidence, and compliance audit posture.

## Strategic domains

### 1. Signals intelligence, signal assurance, and cryptographic resilience

PULVINI's Hilbert-space operator execution, spectral Hamiltonian projection, phi-resonance scoring, and topological anchoring are naturally relevant to high-dimensional signal environments where persistent structure must be distinguished from noise.

The defensible positioning is not offensive collection or operational targeting. The defensible positioning is signal assurance, resilient pattern extraction, high-dimensional anomaly detection, and cryptographic-resilience research.

PYTHAGORAS's QUBO optimisation infrastructure is relevant to lattice-style optimisation problems and post-quantum validation research. The external-facing claim should focus on cryptographic assurance, validation, and resilience testing rather than offensive cryptanalysis.

### 2. Autonomous-system safety assurance

COGITO is HYBA's topological safety-verification layer for autonomous systems. The relevant national-security use case is formal assurance: proving that an autonomous system remains outside prohibited runtime states under defined assumptions.

The defensible positioning is formal safety verification, rules-of-engagement assurance, constrained autonomy, and certification evidence. It should not be positioned as weapons optimisation or autonomous targeting.

EUCLID's topology and quantum-walk execution can support multi-agent coordination analysis and distributed sensor-network resilience under interference, but the public-facing claim should remain safety, resilience, and assurance oriented.

### 3. Intelligence fusion and causal discovery

Metis's causal inference layer is relevant to all-source intelligence fusion because analysts need causal structure, not only correlation.

The defensible positioning is anticipatory analysis, causal discovery, uncertainty-aware fusion, and decision-support evidence. The company should avoid claims of automated conclusions or autonomous operational decisions.

PULVINI's compression and substrate-agnostic execution are relevant to edge environments where compute, bandwidth, and connectivity are constrained. The defensible positioning is portable high-assurance inference and edge-resilient decision support.

### 4. Critical infrastructure protection

PROMETHEUS and GAEA create a natural bridge into grid resilience, infrastructure stability, climate-security foresight, and cascade-failure anticipation.

The defensible positioning is defensive infrastructure hardening, resilience modelling, failure-mode analysis, and intervention planning under transparent human authority.

### 5. Classified-environment AI integrity and zero-trust verification

Themis provides governance and compliance evidence: watermarking, audit chains, claim-boundary controls, documentation, and compliance posture.

Al Haras provides the security enforcement layer: protected execution clearance, runtime security boundaries, tamper-evident decision records, and fail-closed protected handoff.

Together, they support classified-environment AI integrity without collapsing governance and security into one system.

## Export-control and classification boundary

National-security work can trigger export-control, classification, procurement, and dual-use obligations.

Legal review is required before any formal engagement involving:

- classified deployment context
- weapons-adjacent adaptation
- customer-specific autonomous-system integration
- cryptographic evaluation beyond defensive validation
- restricted data environments
- cross-border transfer of technical material
- government procurement representations

The mathematics may be unrestricted. The deployment context may not be.

This review must happen before the first formal government or defence-prime conversation.

## Language to avoid

Avoid absolute or unsupported security claims. Avoid implying autonomous targeting, weapons optimisation, offensive collection, offensive cryptanalysis, or regulatory approval.

Use evidence-backed language tied to named HYBA subsystems, defined threat models, formal assumptions, tests, and reviewable artefacts.
