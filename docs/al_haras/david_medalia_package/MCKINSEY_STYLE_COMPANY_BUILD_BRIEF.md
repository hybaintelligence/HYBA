# Al Haras Company Build Brief

## McKinsey-style executive brief for David Medalia

### BLUF

Al Haras should be built as HYBA's sovereign-grade security company: a focused, high-assurance business protecting HYBA, sovereign clients, critical infrastructure, and national-security-adjacent AI deployments.

A strategy firm would not set this up as a broad consultancy. It would set it up as a category-defining security platform company with three reinforcing business lines:

1. Sovereign AI security assurance
2. Critical-infrastructure and classified-environment protection
3. Formal safety and protected-execution products

The reason is structural. HYBA already has rare substrate capabilities: EUCLID, PULVINI, PYTHAGORAS, COGITO, Metis, THEMIS, and the AL HARAS runtime. The company build should convert these into a focused market vehicle, a repeatable offer catalogue, and a credible assurance posture.

## Strategic question

How should HYBA create a company around security capabilities that conventional cyber, AI, and defence-technology companies cannot easily match?

## Answer

Create Al Haras as an independent but HYBA-controlled sovereign security company with:

- a narrow security-only mandate
- a board-approved claim boundary
- a defensive national-security positioning
- a small elite founding team
- controlled client-entry pathways
- export-control and classification review from day one
- productized assurance artefacts, not bespoke slides
- evidence-backed demonstrations using HYBA's existing stack

## Why now

Three market shifts create timing advantage:

1. Sovereign AI demand is moving from policy to infrastructure.
2. Critical infrastructure operators need AI-enabled resilience but cannot accept black-box autonomy.
3. Defence and intelligence clients need mathematically grounded assurance, not just cybersecurity monitoring.

Al Haras is credible because HYBA has already built the underlying substrate. The task is company formation, packaging, assurance, and controlled market entry.

## Strategic position

Al Haras should be positioned as:

> The sovereign-grade security company for mathematically assured AI, critical infrastructure, and protected execution.

The positioning must avoid unsupported absolutes. It should claim evidence-backed security assurance, not perfection.

## What makes the capability difficult to replicate

### 1. HYBA substrate depth

Conventional cyber companies operate at the network, endpoint, identity, or monitoring layer. Al Haras can operate deeper: at the mathematical substrate, execution boundary, audit-chain, model lineage, and protected-runtime level.

### 2. Substrate independence

PULVINI and HYBA's operator execution layer reduce dependency on one hardware stack, cloud provider, or accelerator vendor. That is strategically valuable for sovereign clients that cannot rely entirely on foreign or single-vendor infrastructure.

### 3. Formal assurance orientation

COGITO and PYTHAGORAS enable a language of proof, invariants, optimisation, and safety certificates. This is stronger than probabilistic monitoring alone.

### 4. Enterprise-stack collapse

Metis compresses fragmented enterprise workflows into a governed intelligence operating layer. Al Haras can secure that collapsed stack at the execution boundary rather than trying to police dozens of tools separately.

### 5. Governance-security separation

Themis handles governance and compliance. Al Haras handles security. This separation is important for serious clients because it prevents claim confusion and supports auditability.

## Principal risks

Mitigation: all claims tied to evidence, tests, assumptions, and named code paths.

### Risk 2: Governance/security confusion

Mitigation: Themis owns governance/compliance; Al Haras owns security.

### Risk 3: Export-control exposure

Mitigation: legal review before formal national-security or defence engagement.

### Risk 4: Becoming a consultancy

Mitigation: productize assurance artefacts and security modules from the start.

### Risk 5: Weapons-adjacent ambiguity

Mitigation: defensive assurance positioning; no autonomous targeting, weapons optimisation, or offensive collection positioning.
