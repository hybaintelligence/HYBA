# Compliance Guidelines

Operating HYBA_FULLSTACK involves cryptocurrency mining, data processing, and potentially financial transactions.  To remain compliant with legal and regulatory requirements, follow these guidelines.

## Legal and regulatory considerations

1. **Know your jurisdiction** – Laws governing cryptocurrency mining and trading vary widely.  Research local regulations, licensing requirements, and tax obligations.
2. **AML/KYC obligations** – If the system interacts with external users or handles cryptocurrency payouts, implement anti‑money‑laundering (AML) and know‑your‑customer (KYC) processes where required.
3. **Financial reporting** – Maintain accurate records of mining revenue, expenses, and taxes.  Consult with a certified accountant knowledgeable in cryptocurrency regulations.
4. **Data protection** – Comply with data‑protection regulations (e.g., GDPR, CCPA) when processing personal data.  Minimise data collection and implement strong access controls.
5. **Environmental regulations** – Some jurisdictions limit energy consumption for mining.  Obtain necessary permits and monitor compliance with energy caps or carbon‑emissions regulations.

## Internal policies

- **Access control** – Grant system access only to authorised personnel.  Use role‑based permissions and audit logs.
- **Secret management** – Use a secret manager to store credentials and rotate them regularly.  Document access policies and review them periodically.
- **Change management** – Implement a change‑control process for code, configuration, and infrastructure updates.  Require peer review and approval.
- **Incident response** – Maintain an incident response plan (see runbook templates) and report breaches to relevant authorities within legal timeframes.

## Auditing and record keeping

- **Logs and metrics** – Keep logs of system activity, API calls, and security events.  Retain logs in accordance with legal requirements.
- **Evidence capture** – Preserve evidence from readiness gates, security scans, and incident reports as part of compliance documentation.
- **Third‑party audits** – Consider engaging external auditors to review security, financial, and environmental compliance.  Address findings promptly.

Compliance is an ongoing process.  Assign ownership to a compliance officer or team and review this document regularly in light of evolving regulations.
