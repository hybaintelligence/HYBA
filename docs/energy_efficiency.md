# Energy Efficiency and Sustainability Guidance

Cryptocurrency mining consumes significant energy.  To operate HYBA_FULLSTACK responsibly and sustainably:

## Monitoring energy usage

- **Measure power consumption** – Deploy power meters at mining rigs and collect energy use metrics.  Aggregate consumption across machines to understand total load.
- **Track efficiency (J/Hash)** – Monitor joules per hash over time.  Identify regressions or improvements after software or hardware changes.
- **Set maximum power budgets** – Use the `HYBA_AUTONOMOUS_MAX_POWER_WATTS` configuration to cap energy use.  Adjust this value based on site capacity and environmental considerations.

## Optimisation strategies

- **Hardware tuning** – Optimise voltage and frequency settings to achieve the best efficiency.  Ensure cooling systems are adequate to prevent thermal throttling.
- **Algorithm efficiency** – Use PHI‑guided search strategies to reduce wasted hash computations and monitor the impact of compression and caching modules.
- **Dynamic scaling** – Configure the reflexive loop to reduce hashrate during low‑profit periods or when energy prices are high.
- **Renewable energy** – When possible, power miners with renewable sources (solar, wind) or participate in green energy programmes.

## Environmental responsibility

- **Offset carbon footprint** – Purchase carbon offsets or credits proportional to energy consumed.
- **Comply with regulations** – Understand local laws regarding energy usage and cryptocurrency mining.  Some jurisdictions require permits or limit mining activities.
- **Public transparency** – Share efficiency metrics and sustainability efforts with stakeholders to build trust and encourage responsible practices.

Energy efficiency is not only a cost consideration but a social and environmental obligation.  Incorporate these principles into operational planning and continuous improvement.
