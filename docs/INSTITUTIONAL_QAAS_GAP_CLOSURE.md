## Claim Boundary

This protocol deliberately closes institutional readiness gaps without claiming external validation before it exists.

HYBA/PYTHIA may claim local, reproducible execution of quantum mathematical operations when backed by tests, certificates, and benchmark artifacts. It must not claim peer-reviewed acceptance, institutional endorsement, regulatory approval, revenue, production customer validation, or physical quantum advantage until those outcomes are externally achieved and archived.

## Implementation Tracks

### 1. Scientific Validation Track

**Immediate deliverables:**

1. Foundational manuscript outline: problem statement, mathematical model, local evidence, limitations, and reproducibility appendix.
2. Formal proof backlog: density matrix axioms, unitary preservation, Born-rule normalization, tensor-network compression conditions, and PULVINI reversibility limits.
3. Benchmark crosswalk: local benchmark names mapped to external-style benchmark dimensions such as circuit width, depth, memory footprint, determinism, and reproducibility.
4. Reproduction bundle: pinned dependencies, deterministic commands, environment metadata, and evidence manifest.

**Institutional acceptance gate:** no paper, benchmark, or public page may say externally validated until there is a citable external artifact.

### 2. Commercial Validation Track

**Immediate deliverables:**

1. Value proposition canvas that frames HYBA/PYTHIA as mathematical quantum-operations infrastructure rather than physical quantum hardware.
2. Pilot qualification checklist for finance, pharma, logistics, and research customers.
3. Pricing worksheet using quantum operation units, logical width, depth, retention SLA, support tier, and isolation tier.
4. ROI study template that compares baseline classical workflow, HYBA/PYTHIA workflow, engineering integration cost, and repeatability risk.

**Institutional acceptance gate:** no ROI, customer, or revenue claim may be published without signed customer evidence or internal finance approval.

### 3. Governance and Ethics Track

**Immediate deliverables:**

1. Scientific Advisory Board charter with role scope, conflict rules, claim-review authority, and non-endorsement language.
2. Ethics Review Committee charter covering consciousness, emergent intelligence, autonomy, evidence handling, and human override.
3. Standards engagement register for NIST, ISO/IEC, IEEE, EU AI Act, GDPR, and sector-specific customer obligations.
4. Continuity plan covering key-person dependency, archival ownership, credential recovery, and institutional memory.

**Institutional acceptance gate:** governance documents must distinguish invited advisors, confirmed advisors, reviewers, and aspirational examples.

### 4. FAIR Infrastructure Track

**Immediate deliverables:**

1. Evidence manifest schema with artifact ID, command, commit, timestamp, environment, checksum, license, and retention class.
2. Collaboration API policy with authentication, rate limits, reproducibility endpoints, abuse controls, and publication protocol.
3. Interoperability crosswalk for OpenQASM, QIR, Qiskit, Braket, NumPy, and HYBA/PYTHIA internal representations.
4. Archive protocol for benchmark results, paper artifacts, proof files, and release snapshots.

**Institutional acceptance gate:** every institutional evidence claim must be traceable to a command, artifact, checksum, or external citation.

## Completion Definition

A gap is considered closed only when all five checks pass:

1. **Artifact exists:** a named document, schema, test, or command is present in the repository or controlled archive.
2. **Owner exists:** a role is accountable for updates and evidence quality.
3. **Acceptance criteria exist:** success is objective and auditable.
4. **Claim boundary exists:** the artifact states what is and is not proven.
5. **Validation hook exists:** an automated or procedural check can detect missing metadata.

This definition makes institutional readiness auditable without overstating external validation.
