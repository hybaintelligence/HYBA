## THINGS TO AVOID

❌ Don't say "We're like Quantum Computing but..."
✓ DO say "Quantum computing was a distraction. We're post-quantum mathematics."

❌ Don't use technical jargon without explaining
✓ DO use: φ-resonance, golden ratio, autonomous, deterministic

❌ Don't sound uncertain
✓ DO sound confident: "We've validated this. The tests prove it."

❌ Don't oversell
✓ DO let the numbers speak: 53x faster, +40% Sharpe, £3-5M/month

❌ Don't forget their business
✓ DO tie everything back: "This solves your £100B portfolio problem"

## BODY LANGUAGE & TONE

**DO:**
- Sit up straight (you're presenting institutional-grade work)
- Make eye contact (even on Zoom)
- Speak slowly (not rushed, not patronizing)
- Pause after key points (let them absorb)
- Listen more than you talk (they want to ask questions)
- Take notes (show you care about their specific concerns)

**DON'T:**
- Rush through materials (30 min goes fast)
- Apologize for the technology ("I know this is weird...")
- Use filler words ("um", "like", "you know")
- Interrupt their questions
- Get defensive if they're skeptical
